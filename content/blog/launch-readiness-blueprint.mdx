## Align stakeholders early

Enterprise launches involve legal, marketing, support, compliance, and executive sponsors. Treat the launch as a project within your project—assign an owner, map stakeholders, and schedule readiness checkpoints.

Create a shared workspace where each team tracks their tasks. Include decision logs so late joiners can catch up quickly.

## Harden the rollout plan

Every enterprise release should include:

- Clear gating criteria and rollback steps.
- Communications plan covering customers, internal teams, and partners.
- Support playbooks with escalation paths and SLAs.

Use dry runs to validate each step. Document who signs off and when.

## Validate compliance and security

Work with security and compliance partners to review data flows, retention policies, and required attestations. For regulated customers, prepare evidence packages ahead of launch day.

## Measure readiness with confidence scores

At Acme, we score readiness across five pillars: product quality, enablement, operations, compliance, and messaging. Each owner grades their pillar green, yellow, or red with context. Leadership reviews the scores during the final go/no-go meeting.

## Celebrate learnings

After launch, run a retrospective focused on learnings for the next cycle. Capture what helped the team stay aligned and where friction appeared. Share outcomes broadly—celebrating wins and transparency builds trust for future releases.
